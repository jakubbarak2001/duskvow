# Landing Copy Pivot — Part 2: Remaining Sections

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Status:** Options for review (no code edits yet)
**Scope:** `frontend/src/app/page.tsx` sections below the hero — the "A Different Oath" block, "The Rite" walkthrough, the Final CTA, and the footer tagline.

## Why Part 2 exists

The hero was pivoted from *aesthetic-as-pitch* to *mechanic-as-pitch* with a caring-guide voice. The rest of the page still reads in the old voice — the Anti-Section literally mocks *"gentle reminders"* (the exact emotional quality the Healthy Gamer signal praised), and the footer tagline closes on *"solitude,"* which is the opposite of the new *"you don't walk it alone"* positioning.

Guiding principle for every line below: **would the user who said *"feels like they want me to get better"* read this and still feel that way?** If not, it's off-brand now.

Secondary discipline carried over from Part 1: *"forge"* is a finite resource on this page. The hero H1 already spends it (*"We'll Forge the Path"*). Nothing below should spend it again — it becomes a tic. So Step II's *"forged from your goal"* is in scope for a swap too, even though it wasn't flagged.

## Section 1 — "A Different Oath" block (the Anti-Section)

### 1a. Eyebrow (currently: "A DIFFERENT OATH")

Keep. *"A Different Oath"* is the one line in the whole section that isn't attacking anyone — *different* implies contrast without naming the thing contrasted. It also sets up the vow metaphor that pays off at the final CTA. Two alternatives if you want more warmth:

- **"A Different Oath"** *(keep)* — consistent, tribal, doesn't pick a fight
- **"The Oath"** — drops the comparative framing entirely; pure declaration
- **"What We Promise"** — warmest; works if the new body is a genuine promise

### 1b. Struck-through list (currently: "Cute companions." / "8-bit sprites." / "Gentle reminders.")

Two directions to choose between.

**Direction A — Keep the strike device, swap targets to generic SaaS tropes.** Safer rewrite; keeps visual continuity.

- Option A1:
  > ~~Another dashboard.~~
  > ~~Another streak counter.~~
  > ~~Another productivity hack.~~
- Option A2:
  > ~~Quarterly reviews.~~
  > ~~Motivation quotes.~~
  > ~~Accountability scoreboards.~~

Both strike at corporate/generic-productivity tropes instead of anyone's warmth. A1 uses "another" to suggest fatigue; A2 strikes at the sterile/corporate register.

**Direction B — Replace the strike with a positive three-beat list.** More ambitious. Drops the "we are not X" device entirely, which is the clearest way to escape the section's old frame.

- Option B1 (tools/lore/bonfire echo):
  > A blade that fits your hand.
  > Lore that speaks to your goal.
  > A bonfire when you rest.
- Option B2 (vow/path/hearth):
  > A vow you make out loud.
  > A path shaped to your goal.
  > A hearth that waits for your return.
- Option B3 (simpler, rhythmic):
  > Your vow.
  > Your path.
  > Your hearth.

B1 directly maps to the "these games give you tools, lore, and bonfires" frame from the brief — it's the most conceptually faithful translation. B3 is the tightest if the section needs to breathe.

### 1c. Headline (currently: "We built for the rest of you.")

The replacement needs to declare what Duskvow *is*, not what it excludes.

- Option H1: **"We built for the goal you can't put down."** — centers the user's commitment, not the product's toughness. Warm but serious.
- Option H2: **"We built the forge. You swing the hammer."** — shared authorship. You supply the goal; we supply the structure. *(Carries "forge" — only use if you drop it from the hero, which we don't recommend.)*
- Option H3: **"We built the path. You walk it. We walk it with you."** — most explicit on the guide note. Three beats, mentor voice.
- Option H4: **"We built a place to come back to."** — leans into the hearth/bonfire metaphor. Retention-forward.
- Option H5: **"We built it for the long walk."** — acknowledges self-improvement is not a sprint, without guilt-tripping.

### 1d. Body paragraph (currently: Malenia/Spire → "a system built for people who don't need to be coddled.")

Reframe target: *these games respect you because they give you tools, lore, and bonfires. The mentorship is the respect.*

- Option B-α (closest to the original structure):
  > You didn't beat Malenia by toughing it out. You had a flask, a map, and a grace to return to. You didn't clear the Spire by grit alone — you had a deck, a map, and a fire to camp by. Your real-life goals deserve the same: **tools to use, a path to read, and a place to rest.**

- Option B-β (shorter, tighter):
  > Malenia falls because Elden Ring gives you runes, a map, and a grace to rest at. The Spire falls because the game gives you a deck, a map, and a fire. Respect, in those games, looks like scaffolding. **We think your real-life goals deserve the same kind.**

- Option B-γ (most mentor-voiced):
  > The games you love don't respect you by hurting you. They respect you by giving you what you need to keep going — a blade, a map, a bonfire, and something waiting on the other side of the boss. **We tried to build the self-improvement app that respects you the same way.**

All three keep the Malenia/Spire references as tribal signals but reframe what they *prove.* B-α is the most incremental edit (same rhythm as the original sentence-by-sentence). B-γ is the biggest voice pivot (first-person-plural-humble: "We tried to build").

## Section 2 — "The Rite" walkthrough

Step headlines (*"Speak Your Goal"*, *"Receive Your Tree"*, *"Walk the Path"*) stay. They're distinctive, rhythmic, and on-brand. Only sublines change.

### Step I — currently: *"Enter any ambition. The AI listens."*

The verb *listens* is already good; *the AI* is the cold word. Reframe the subject.

- Option I-1: **"Say what you're chasing. We read every word before we build."**
- Option I-2: **"Tell us what you want. We listen like it matters — because it does."**

I-1 is cleaner and doesn't ask the copy to do emotional work the user isn't yet ready for. I-2 is warmer but risks sentimentality on first contact.

### Step II — currently: *"A branching talent tree, forged from your goal."*

Swap *forged* (locked to the hero) for a different verb. Candidates: *shaped, grown, drawn, charted.*

- Option II-1: **"A branching talent tree, shaped from the words you spoke."**
- Option II-2: **"A living talent tree, grown from your goal."**

II-1 links back to Step I by referencing "the words you spoke" — creates a small narrative thread across the three steps. II-2 leans into *living* and *grown* as biological metaphors, which fits trees literally.

### Step III — currently: *"Complete nodes. Earn XP. Watch your tree ignite."*

*Node* is internal vocabulary (same note from Part 1's reviewer). Also: this is the step where the companionship hint should land.

- Option III-1: **"Walk it at your pace. Earn XP as you go. The path lights behind you."**
- Option III-2: **"Take each step when you're ready. Mark each win. The ember carries forward with you."**

III-1 is tighter and keeps the XP mechanic visible. III-2 drops "XP" (which is acceptable since the full page already mentions it in the subhead and the tree itself shows it) in favor of "mark each win" + the ember/companionship beat. Both avoid *nodes*.

## Section 3 — Final CTA block

Keep the headline (*"Every Journey Begins With a Vow"*) and the main subhead (*"Free. No trials. No tricks. One goal, one tree, one path forward."*). These are already working.

### 3a. Micro-copy under the button (currently: *"Free forever. No tricks. No trials. Just the path."*)

Problem: *"No trials. No tricks."* appears in the subhead one line above. Don't say it twice. Make this line do a *different* job — the emotional closer.

- Option MC-1: **"Begin when you're ready. The path waits."** — patient mentor voice; two short beats
- Option MC-2: **"Start in a minute. Stay as long as it serves you."** — practical trust signal + permission to leave
- Option MC-3: **"We'll be here when you're ready."** — warmest; drops the trust-signal job entirely and leaves it to the subhead

All three assume the subhead is already carrying *"free / no tricks / no trials."* MC-1 is the most balanced.

### 3b. Footer tagline (currently: *"Forged in solitude. Tempered by ambition."*)

The single most important line on this page to rewrite. *"Solitude"* directly contradicts the hero's *"you don't walk it alone."* Also: *"Forged"* is locked to the hero — replace, don't repeat.

- Option F-1: **"Tempered by ambition. Tended by hand."** — preserves half the original cadence (*"Tempered by ambition"*), swaps *"Forged in solitude"* for the mentor verb *"Tended."* Minimal change, maximum pivot.
- Option F-2: **"An oath made alone. A journey walked together."** — tells a small story across two beats. Explicitly resolves the solitude-vs-companionship tension the old line created.
- Option F-3: **"One vow kindled. One hearth kept."** — all-new imagery (kindled ember + kept hearth). Most atmospheric; least continuous with the old line.
- Option F-4: **"Lit by the ember. Walked together."** — simplest; the "Walked together" is the direct inverse of "in solitude."
- Option F-5: **"The ember remembers. The path waits."** — pure mentor voice; no first-person, no second-person. Closes the page on patience.

## Recommended combination

### Section 1 — Anti-Section
- **Eyebrow:** keep *"A Different Oath"*
- **Struck-through list:** **Direction B, Option B1** (blade / lore / bonfire). This is the single biggest lift — moves from "we are not cute" to "we are this." It maps cleanly onto the Malenia/Spire reframe below, so the strike-through list and the body paragraph are saying the same thing in different registers.
- **Headline:** **H3 — *"We built the path. You walk it. We walk it with you."*** — the mentor voice at full volume. Pairs naturally with the hero's *"we'll forge the path."*
- **Body:** **B-α** (closest structural edit). Keeps the Malenia/Spire rhythm the page already has, just swaps what the metaphor proves.

### Section 2 — The Rite
- Step I: **I-1** *"Say what you're chasing. We read every word before we build."*
- Step II: **II-1** *"A branching talent tree, shaped from the words you spoke."*
- Step III: **III-1** *"Walk it at your pace. Earn XP as you go. The path lights behind you."*

This trio creates a narrative thread — *you speak words → we shape a tree from those words → you walk it, and the path lights behind you.* Each step hands off to the next.

### Section 3 — Final CTA + Footer
- **Micro-copy:** **MC-1** — *"Begin when you're ready. The path waits."*
- **Footer tagline:** **F-1** — *"Tempered by ambition. Tended by hand."*

### Why this combination

The Anti-Section stops being an attack and starts being a *manifesto.* The strike-through list names three on-brand things Duskvow provides (blade/lore/bonfire), the headline declares *"we walk it with you,"* and the body paragraph keeps the Elden Ring / Slay the Spire tribal signals intact but reframes their meaning — these games respect the player by giving them *scaffolding,* not by withholding help. That is the exact claim Duskvow needs to make for itself.

The Rite's three sublines then read as a small story across the page: *say what you're chasing* → *we shape a tree from your words* → *you walk it at your pace, and the path lights behind you.* Every step includes the user, never the app alone, which is the same voice the hero now uses.

The final CTA keeps its strongest lines (*"Every Journey Begins With a Vow"*) and adds a patient closer: *"Begin when you're ready. The path waits."* The footer tagline is the last impression the visitor leaves with — *"Tempered by ambition. Tended by hand"* preserves the gravitas of the original (the *"Tempered by ambition"* half) but replaces the single word that was doing the most emotional damage (*solitude* → *tended by hand*). One hand-edit; the whole footer stops contradicting the rest of the page.
